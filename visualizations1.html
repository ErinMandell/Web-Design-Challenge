Points Scored per Season



The total average points scored per player each season peaked in 1967 at 735 points, and then saw a gradual decline in the '70's, '80's, and 90's. The average points scored then leveled out starting around the year 2000 to a range of 450-500. A quick look at the number of minutes played each season shows us why. The average minutes played per season moved in direct correlation to the points scored.

A further look into the data to determine why the number of minutes played has decreased since 1969 reveals that the average number of games played per player also follows this same pattern. This is true even though the number of games per season has been constant at 82 since the 1967-68 season.

Note: There was a lockout during the 1998-99 season, so only 50 games were played that year.
