Average Points Scored by Position



In it's earlier days, basketball was a game where the 'big man' was typcially the star of the show. The game was slower in those days, with teams taking their time to set up plays and work the ball into the center. Introduction of the shot clock in 1954 doesn't seem to have affected this much, but strategy and style evolved over the late 60's and 70's nonetheless, with guards ultimately taking the lead on points scored in the 2000's - especially with the boost that higher shooting percentage on three-pointers gave them.

These are interesting times for the Centers once again... Lines are being blurred between a player's position and his predetermined role in the game. Centers are moving around the court more, and the extraordinary height and athleticism of players joining the league from around the world is pushing the game in new directions.

Note: There was a lockout during the 1998-99 season, so only 50 games were played that year.
